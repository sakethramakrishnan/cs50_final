Solving Quadratics using the Quadratic Formula

The quadratic formula provides the solution(s) to a quadratic equation \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \). The formula is:

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

Follow these steps:

  1. Identify the coefficients \( a \), \( b \), and \( c \) from the quadratic equation.
  2. Substitute the coefficients into the quadratic formula.
  3. Calculate the discriminant \( b^2 - 4ac \).
  4. Determine the roots based on the discriminant value.


For example, take the equation \( x^2 - 3x + 2 = 0 \)

The coefficients are \( a = 1 \), \( b = -3 \), and \( c = 2 \). Substituting these into the quadratic formula gives:

\[ x = \frac{-(-3) \pm \sqrt{(-3)^2 - 4(1)(2)}}{2(1)} = \frac{3 \pm \sqrt{1}}{2} \]

This results in two solutions: \( x = 2 \) and \( x = 1 \).
