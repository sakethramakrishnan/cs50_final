Solving Quadratics by Factorizing

Factorizing involves breaking down the polynomial into simpler terms that can be multiplied to get the original polynomial. Follow these steps:

  1. Write the polynomial in standard form.
  2. Identify common factors and group terms.
  3. Use factoring techniques such as factoring by grouping, difference of squares, or trinomial factoring.
  4. Set each factor equal to zero and solve for the roots.


For example, to factorize the polynomial \( x^2 - 5x + 6 \), we find:

\[ x^2 - 5x + 6 = (x - 2)(x - 3) \]

Setting each factor to zero gives us the roots:

\[ x - 2 = 0 \quad \text{or} \quad x - 3 = 0 \]

Thus, the solutions are \( x = 2 \) and \( x = 3 \).
