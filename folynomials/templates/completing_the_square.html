Solving Quadratics by Completing the Square

Completing the square is a method to solve quadratic equations. Follow these steps:

  1. Write the quadratic equation in the form \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \).
  2. Move the constant term to the other side of the equation.
  3. Add and subtract the square of half the coefficient of \( x \) to complete the square.
  4. Factor the perfect square trinomial.
  5. Take the square root of both sides and solve for \( x \).


For example, consider the equation \( x^2 + 6x + 5 = 0 \):

  1. Move the constant term: \( x^2 + 6x = -5 \)
  2. Complete the square: \( x^2 + 6x + 9 = 4 \)
  3. Factor the perfect square trinomial: \( (x + 3)^2 = 4 \)
  4. Solve for \( x \):    \( x + 3 = \pm 2 \)

Thus, the solutions are \( x = -1 \) and \( x = -5 \).
